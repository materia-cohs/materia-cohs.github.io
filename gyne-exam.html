Gynecological
Examination

Preparation
  • If male examiner, request for a chaperone to be present.
  • Wash hands, wear your gloves.
  • Explain the steps of the examination to the patient as this reduces anxiety and enhances cooperation
  • Obtain consent
  • The patient should be advised to void as an empty bladder promotes comfort and allows for more productive examination.
  • Provision of privacy
  • Prepare the equipment.
  • Position the patient
General physical examination

General observation/appearance

  • Is the patient in any apparent distress?
  • Is she drowsy/unconscious; not oriented to time, place and person?
  • Lying/sitting comfortably (with IV cannula in rt. /lt. hand/Foley’s catheter in situ)

Body mass index (BMI)

  • Height
  • Weight
  • BMI (Weight in kg/height2 in m2)

Eye

  • Conjuctival pallor for anemia
  • Scleral jaundice

Mouth

  • Mouth hygiene, caries, and denture
  • Dorsum of the tongue- dehydration, leukoplakia
  • Tip of the tongue- central cyanosis
  • Ventral surface of the tongue- buccal mucosa pallor
  • Buccal mucosa for cyanosis and dehydration

Neck

  • Inspect the neck from the front for any obvious swelling. If there is a swelling, perform a proper thyroid examination.
  • Lymph node enlargement (cervical and supraclavicular LN).

Hand

  • Palmar crease for pallor
  • Capillary refill
  • Finger clubbing
  • Repeat on contralateral hand

Pedal edema

  • Posterior aspect of the lateral malleolus.
  • Press for 15 seconds.
  • Check for dimpling. If it dimples, move to the tibia, then knee, then sacrum.

Varicose veins and skeletal abnormalities.

Use PILCCED for easy recall when recording- Pallor, Icterus, Lymph nodes, Cyanosis, Clubbing, Edema, Dehydration status.

Vital signs
  • Pulse- x/min
  • Respiratory rate- x breadths/min
  • SPO2
  • Temperature- x oC
  • Blood pressure- x/y mmHg- should be measured in a semi-recumbent position with an appropriate cuff.
CVS & RS examination

If indicated. Check here for cardiovascular examination and here for respiratory examination.

You can auscultate the heart and lungs- cardiac murmurs may be detected for the first time in pregnancy.

Abdominal examination
  • Ask the patient to lie supine with arms at their side and support the head by pillows.
  • Expose the abdomen from the sternum to the pubic symphysis. The groin should be visible and the genitalia should remain covered.
  • Placing a pillow beneath the knees or semiflexed the hip and knee often aids the relaxation of abdominal muscles.

Inspection

  • Shape- abdomen contour normal, distension (generalized or localized) or sunken (scaphoid).
  • Movement of abdominal wall – movement with respiration, symmetrical or not.
  • Umbilicus- inverted or everted and slit like (circular, transvers or vertical).
  • Any visible peristalsis or pulsation.
  • Venous prominence, striae, scar marks, discoloration, rashes, scratch marks.
  • Hernia sites (inguinal, femoral, umbilical and paraumbilical) – ask the patient to cough while inspecting hernia sites.
  • Any localized swelling/mass.

Palpation

Superficial palpation:

  • Ask the patient if they feel pain anywhere in the abdomen. The region of pain should be palpated last.
  • Palpate all 9 regions of the abdomen.
  • For tenderness- generalized or localized.
  • Always observe the patient’s face for signs of discomfort.

Deep palpation:

  • for organomegaly
    • Hepatomegaly- start from the right iliac fossa and move upwards.
    • Splenomegaly- start from the right iliac fossa and move diagonally upwards to the left hypochondriac region.
  • Uterine/ovarian mass (site, size, shape, surface, tenderness, consistency, mobility, edges, attachment).
  • Rebound tenderness.
  • Ballot for the kidneys.

Percussion

  • General abdomen- should be resonant.
  • Shifting dullness- if the abdomen is distended.

Auscultation

  • Bowel sounds
  • Uterine soufflé- vascular fibroid.
Pelvic examination
  • Explain the procedure to patient and take the consent. Ensure their privacy.
  • Ask the patient to empty her bladder before examination.
  • Ask the patient to remove their cloths from waist downwards
  • Patient position: lie on her back with both hips and knees flexed and both thighs abducted.
  • Wear your gloves.

Inspection of the vulva (external genitalia)

  • Inspect the Mons and note the pattern of hair distribution.
  • Separate the labia from above with the forefinger and thumb of your left hand.
  • Look for any anatomical abnormalities.
  • Note any visible vaginal discharge- color, odor, character.
  • Inspect the skin of external genitalia for- inflammation, swelling, atrophy, ulceration, warts, scars, pigmentation, other skin lesion.
  • Clitoris- size and any lesion
  • External urethral meatus- inflammation, discharge, any mass.
  • Visible opening of Bartholin’s duct (normally not visible unless inflammed), or any swelling.
  • Character of hymen.
  • Ask the patient to strain or cough to elicit-
    • Stress incontinence
    • Genital prolapse- structures involved (anterior vaginal wall, uterus alone, posterior vaginal wall, all three)

Palpation

  • Palpate Bartholin gland area (normally not palpable)
  • For tenderness, cyst or abscess
  • Press urethra from above down to note any discharge
  • Lesions- if present

Per speculum examination

  • Insect cervix- shape of external os, color, redness, discharge, ulcer, erosion, polyp, bleeding on touch, mass.
  • Vagina- color, ulcer, discharge, mass.

Bimanual examination

  • Separate the labia with the thumb and forefinger of the gloved left hand to expose the introitus.
  • Lubricate your index finger and middle fingers of the right hand with jelly and gently introduce the fingers into the vagina with the fingers palm facing laterally and then hand is rotated 90o so that the palm faces upwards.
  • The thumb should be abducted and the ring and little finger flexed into the palm.
  • Feel the vaginal wall for tenderness and nodularity.
  • Cervix- cervical os (open or closed), position, consistency, motion/mobility, tenderness and any pathology.
  • Adnexae- any tenderness, mass (size, shape, consistency, and mobility).
  • Now place the left hand on the abdomen above the pubis symphisis.
  • While you push the cervix and uterus up with your right (vaginal) hand, press your abdominal hand in and down trying to grasp the uterus between your two hands. Note the following-
    • Uterus- size, shape, position, consistency, mobility, tenderness, (normally anteverted, pear shaped, firm, freely mobile in all direction).

Rectal or rectovaginal examination- if indicated.
