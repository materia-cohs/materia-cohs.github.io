History Taking
Gynecology

Biodata

Name, Age, Tribe, Religion, Occupation, Marital status, Address, Educational status (NATROMAE).

Presenting complaints

This should be a brief sentence as to why they have come to the hospital. There may be multiple presenting complaints and they should be mentioned in chronological order with duration.

Check here for common gynecological symptoms and how to clerk them.

History of presenting complaints

If more than one complaint, explore each individually.

Detail of each complaint: duration, mode of onset, progression, periodicity and frequency, associated symptoms, aggravating and relieving factors.

The SOCRATES acronym is a useful tool for exploring each of the patientâ€™s presenting symptoms in more detail. It is most commonly used to explore pain, but it can be applied to other symptoms, although some of the elements of SOCRATES may not be relevant to all symptoms.

SOCRATES stands for Site, Onset, Character, Radiation, Associated symptoms, Time course, Exacerbating and alleviating factors, Severity. Check here for details.

The 5Cs can also be used to explore the presenting complaints:

  • Characterize each symptom
  • Course- progression of symptoms
  • Cause- ask questions to rule out differentials and identify likely causes
  • Complications- complications of the suspected disease process and how they affect the patientâ€™s quality of life.
  • Care received so far- at home, at referral center and since admission

Have relevant differential diagnosis in mind and mention significant negatives e.g. not sexually active in a reproductive age woman with acute abdomen.

Gynecological history

This includes menstrual, sexual and contraceptive, and post-menopausal histories.

  • Age at menarche
  • First day of last menstrual period (LMP)
  • Days of flow
  • Length of menstrual cycle, regular or irregular
  • Menorrhagia/number of pads per day
  • Passage of clots
  • Dysmenorrhea
  • Sexually active?
  • Dyspareunia
  • Post-coital/intermenstrual bleeding
  • Contraceptive use- which method? Still using, stopped, changed? If stop or changed, why? Any side effect encountered? Duration of use and date of last dose taken.
  • Breast symptoms (such as tenderness, discharge, lumps)
  • Previous screening/diagnostic tests such as cervical smear, mammography- date, report, and any treatments received.

If postmenopausal

  • Last menstrual bleeding
  • Postmenopausal bleeding
  • Menopausal symptoms
  • Use of HRT
Past obstetric history
  • Conception difficulties/subfertility.
  • Date/year of pregnancies
  • Outcome- live birth, still birth, neonatal death, miscarriages (period of gestation and complication), termination, (period of gestation, place, the method used, any complications).
  • Pregnancy- duration and any antenatal complication (e.g. PIH/pre-eclampsia/eclampsia, GDM).
  • Labor- induced/spontaneous, normal or any complication (particularly during third stage).
  • Type and method of delivery- ask about indication if CS or instrumental.
  • Puerperium- normal or any complication (e.g. PPH, infection, DVT, etc.).
  • Baby- sex, weight, any complication, breast feeding duration, immunization.
Past medical & surgical history

Infection

  • Any past history of pelvic inflammatory disease (PID).
  • Whether it was adequately treated, including contact tracing.
  • Any known contact with sexually transmitted infections.
  • Assessment of the risk of HIV and hepatitis B.

Any medical illness requiring treatment and/or hospitalization.

Personal history of

  • Diabetes
  • Hypertension
  • Hemoglobinopathies- SCD, thalassemias
  • Cardiac disease
  • Chronic renal disease
  • TB
  • Asthma
  • Epilepsy
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Systemic autoimmune diseases- SLE, rheumatoid arthritis

Ask the patient if they have previously undergone any surgery or procedures in the past, general or gynecological.

Any blood transfusion?

Medications and allergies
  • Prescribed or over the counter medications the patient has been taking since falling pregnant, noting which they are still taking and which they have now stopped.
  • Document the medication name, dose, frequency, form and route, any significant side effect developed.
  • Herbal/complementary therapies
  • History of HRT
  • Allergies- including environmental, food, drug, and their severity/ type of allergic reaction
  • It is important to note if they take their medications correctly or if they do it too often/too little.
Family history
  • History of same illness in the family- if appropriate.
  • Any history of hereditary illnesses or congenital defects, to ensure adequate counselling and screening.
  • Cancers of the reproductive system in the family (e.g., breast cancer and ovarian cancer).
  • Diabetes
  • Hypertension
Social history
  • Smoking- type and amount
  • Alcohol abuse- type and volume on a weekly basis
  • Recreational drug use
  • Diet and weight
  • Relationship status
  • Multiple sexual partners?
  • Impacts of current illness on social life, employment, family life, sexual activity.
Review of systems

General

  • Weight loss, fever.

CNS

  • Headache, fits/faints/dizziness, trauma, vertigo, sleep habits, vision, incoordination.

CVS

  • Chest pain, dyspnea, dyspnea on exertion, orthopnea, PND, palpitations, angina.

RS

  • Cough, sputum/hemoptysis, sore throat/earache, weight loss/night sweats/fever, tuberculosis contact.

GIT

  • Abdominal pain, dyspepsia/indigestion, appetite, dysphagia, nausea/vomiting/hematemesis, change in bowel habit, constipation/diarrhea, nauture of stool.

Urinary system

  • Frequency/nocturia, dysuria, hematuria, Incontinence (stress, urge, overflow), urine color/odor/amount

Locomotor system

  • Muscle weakness/cramps/stiffness, joint pain/swelling/stiffness/deformity.

Skin

  • Rash/sore/itch

  • Two or three sentences

  • Presenting complaints
  • Current situation
  • Actions taken- investigations and plan
