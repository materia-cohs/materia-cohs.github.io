History Taking
Obstetrics

Biodata

Name, Age, Tribe, Religion, Occupation, Marital status, Address, Educational status.

(NATROMAE)

Key pregnancy details

First day of the last menstrual period (LMP), estimated date of delivery (EDD), estimated gestational age (EGA).

  • You should estimate the GA and EDD from their LMP.
  • Establish the patient's certainty of dates, regularity of the cycle and the use of contraceptives. If patient is unsure of date, ask for an early USS report.

Gravidity: the total number of pregnancies, including the current one.

Parity: the number of pregnancy carried beyond the age of viability, which is 28 weeks in this environment.

Recording

  • The terminology used is GxPy+z (a alive)
  • G is gravida, P is para.
  • x is the gravidity, the total number of pregnancies, including the current one.
  • y is the number of pregnancies carried beyond 28 weeks of gestation.
  • z is the number of miscarriages or termination of pregnancies before 28 weeks gestation.
    • a is the number of children alive.

For example, a woman who is pregnant for the 4th time with 1 normal delivery at term that is still alive, 1 termination at 9 weeks, 1 miscarriage at 16 weeks would be gravida 4, para 1+2 (1 alive).

Last child birth- to determine interpregnnacy and interdelivery interval

Presenting complaints

This should be a brief sentence as to why they have come to the hospital. There may be multiple presenting complaints and they should be mentioned in chronological order with duration.

For example,

  • 8 months amenorrhea (LMP, EDD, EGA)
  • Bleeding per vaginam of 2 hour duration

Check here for common obstetric symptoms and how to clerk them.

There may not be any. The patient may have come for antenatal clinic (ANC) booking or a routine antenatal follow up visit.

History of presenting complaints

If more than one complain, explore each individually.

Specific details about the presenting complaints.

The SOCRATES acronym is a useful tool for exploring each of the patient’s presenting symptoms in more detail. It is most commonly used to explore pain, but it can be applied to other symptoms, although some of the elements of SOCRATES may not be relevant to all symptoms.

SOCRATES stands for Site, Onset, Character, Radiation, Associated symptoms, Time course, Exacerbating and alleviating factors, Severity. Check here for details.

The 5Cs can also be used to explore the presenting complaints

  • Characterize each symptom.
  • Course- progression of symptoms.
  • Cause- ask questions to rule out differentials and identify likely causes.
  • Complications- complications of the suspected disease process and how they affect the patient’s quality of life.
  • Care received so far- at home, at referral center and since admission.

Have relevant differential diagnoses in mind mention significant negatives e.g. not sexually active in a reproductive age woman with acute abdomen.

History of current pregnancy

General:

  • Is the patient booked? Where? When? If not, why?
  • Indication at booking.
  • GA at booking.
  • Total ANC visits.
  • Was pregnancy desired? Spontaneously conceived?
  • How pregnancy was suspected and method of confirmation.
  • GA at confirmation.
  • Singleton or multiple.
  • Tetanus toxoid and gestational age received, number of doses received.
  • IPT and gestational age received, number of doses, what drug?
  • Hematinics use- folic acid, fersolate.
  • Use of ARTs.

History of first trimester:

  • Possible problems
    • Bleeding, pain (ectopic pregnancy, miscarriage)
    • Vaginal discharge
    • Hyperemesis
    • Urinary problems
  • Investigations done
  • Medications used for treatment

History of second and third trimesters:

  • Fetal movement, GA at first fetal movement
  • General health- sleep, diet, bowel movement
  • Symptoms of anemia, miscarriage, ectopic pregnancy, vaginal discharge, UTI, hyperemesis gravidarum
  • Symptoms of APH, PIH, DM, preterm labor
  • Results of antenatal investigations including
    • Blood pressure
    • PCV, FBC
    • Urinalysis
    • Blood group
    • Genotype
    • Viral markers- HBSAg, anti HBC, LVS, VDRL
    • Ultrasound scans, latest done
  • Investigations done
  • Medications used for treatment

She may not know the details, ask if the results were normal.

If she is postnatal:

  • Intrapartum and postpartum complications
Past obstetric history

Details of all previous pregnancies, including miscarriages and terminations.

live births:

  • Date (month/year)
  • Mode of delivery- spontaneous vaginal, assisted vaginal or caesarean.
  • Gender
  • Birth weight
  • Any significant antenatal, intra-partum or post-partum events/complications- pre-eclampsia, pregnancy induced hypertension, gestational diabetes, post-partum hemorrhage.
  • If the mother is not aware of previous perinatal complications, try asking her how soon she went home with the baby after the delivery.
  • The quality of care provider and health center.
  • Assisted reproductive therapies (ART), e.g. induction of ovulation with clomiphene. ARTs can increase the risk of pre-eclampsia during pregnancy.
  • Life and health of the child(ren)

Abortions/miscarriages/terminations:

  • Outcome (spontaneous, medical management, surgical management- evacuation of retained productions of conception).
  • Identified causes of miscarriage/intrauterine fetal loss (stillbirth).
  • For ectopic pregnancies, ask about:
    • Site of the ectopic
    • Management
      • Expectant (monitoring of serum hCG levels)
      • Medical (methotrexate injection)
      • Surgical (laparoscopy or laparotomy; salpingectomy or -otomy)
Gynecological history
  • Age at menarche
  • Days of flow
  • Length of menstrual cycle, regular or irregular
  • Menorrhagia/number of pads per day
  • Dysmenorrhea
  • Dyspareunia
  • Post-coital/intermenstrual bleeding
  • Postmenopausal bleeding, if appropriate
  • Contraceptive use before conception, type and duration
  • Cervical pap smear history- date, result
  • History of STD
Past medical & surgical histories
  • Any medical illness requiring treatment and/or hospitalization.
  • Personal history of
    • Diabetes
    • Hypertension
    • Hemoglobinopathies- SCD, thalassemias
    • Cardiac disease
    • Chronic renal disease
    • TB
    • Asthma
    • Epilepsy
    • Hypothyroidism
    • Blood-borne viruses
    • Systemic autoimmune diseases- SLE, rheumatoid arthritis
    • Previous venous thromboembolic events
    • Any other history suggestive of RTI / STI ; HIV / AIDS
  • Ask the patient if they have previously undergone any surgery or procedures in the past such as:
    • Abdominal or pelvic surgery: may influence decisions regarding delivery due to the presence of scar tissue and adhesions.
    • Previous Caesarean section: increased risk of uterine rupture in subsequent pregnancies.
    • Loop excision of the transitional zone (LETZ): increased risk of cervical incompetence.
  • Any blood transfusions.
Medications & allergies
  • Prescribed or over the counter medications the patient has been taking since falling pregnant, noting which they are still taking and which they have now stopped.
  • Document the medication name , dose, frequency, form and route.
  • Herbal/complementary therapies.
  • Allergies and their severity/ type of allergic reaction.
  • It is important to note if they take their medications correctly or if they do it too often/too little.
Family history
  • History of same illness in the family- if appropriate.
  • Any history of hereditary illnesses or congenital defects, to ensure adequate counselling and screening.
  • Cancers of the reproductive system in the family (e.g., breast cancer and ovarian cancer).
  • Diabetes
  • Hypertension
  • Twinning
Social history
  • The type of accommodation and if there are any adaptations to assist them (e.g. stairlift).
  • Who else the patient lives with and their personal support network.
  • What tasks they are able to carry out independently and what they require assistance with (e.g. self-hygiene, housework, food shopping).
  • Smoking- type and amount.
  • Recreational drug use.
  • Diet and weight.
  • Marital setting- poly/monogamous.
  • Age of husband.
  • Husband’s occupation.
  • Husband’s educational status.
  • Domestic violence screening.
Review of systems

General

  • Weight loss, fever.

CNS

  • Headache, fits/faints/dizziness, trauma, vertigo, sleep habits, vision, incoordination.

CVS

  • Chest pain, dyspnea, dyspnea on exertion, orthopnea, PND, palpitations, angina.

RS

  • Cough, sputum/hemoptysis, sore throat/earache, weight loss/night sweats/fever, tuberculosis contact.

GIT

  • Abdominal pain, dyspepsia/indigestion, appetite, dysphagia, nausea/vomiting/hematemesis, change in bowel habit, constipation/diarrhea, nauture of stool.

Urinary system

  • Frequency/nocturia, dysuria, hematuria, Incontinence (stress, urge, overflow), urine color/odor/amount

Locomotor system

  • Muscle weakness/cramps/stiffness, joint pain/swelling/stiffness/deformity.

Skin

  • Rash/sore/itch
Summary
  • Two or three sentences
  • Patient’s name
  • Age
  • EGA
  • Current problem/situation
  • Actions taken- investigations and plan

Example:

I have presented Mrs. AK, a 32 year old booked G3P2+0(2A) primary school teacher with 1 previous caesarean section 3 years ago for persistent breech at term. She presented today for routine antenatal clinic visit at EGA of 20 weeks and has no complaints. She has commenced hematinics.
